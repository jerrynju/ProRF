# Signal Cartography

*A design philosophy for the ProRF interface*

---

## The Movement

Signal Cartography treats the RF engineering workflow as an act of mapmaking — the invisible electromagnetic landscape rendered legible through ordered, precise visual language. Every screen is a territory to be charted: signal flows become rivers, nodes become waypoints, loss and gain become elevation. The designer is a cartographer of energy, and the interface is the map.

---

## Space and Form

Negative space is sovereign. Content breathes inside generous white fields, each element given room to exist without crowding its neighbors. Cards float with restrained elevation — shadows barely whispered, edges crisply defined at 12-pixel radii. The information hierarchy moves from mass to detail: bold summaries anchor the eye first, secondary data surfaces only upon approach. Depth is expressed through layering — bottom sheets ascend from silence, modal panels slide in from the edge, never interrupting but always announcing themselves. Every boundary is earned, not decorated.

---

## Color and Signal

Color is not decoration — it is data. Each node type in the signal chain carries a distinct chromatic identity: the transmitter speaks in deep indigo, the amplifier burns amber, the coupler glows teal, the antenna holds royal violet, free-space propagation settles into forest green, and the receiver rests in slate. These identities remain absolutely consistent across every screen, every panel, every chart, binding the system into a coherent visual language. Values that grow are rendered in verdant green; losses and reductions carry cadmium red. The interface teaches without text — a glance at color tells the story before numbers are read. The palette is meticulously calibrated: never garish, always purposeful, as if mixed by someone who spent countless hours with Albers' color interaction studies before touching a single pixel.

---

## Scale and Rhythm

Typography is a measurement instrument, not mere labeling. Numeric values are displayed in monospace — JetBrains Mono — because measurements demand alignment and uniformity. Section titles use a grotesque bold with engineered precision. Body labels are set in a rational sans-serif at constrained weights, never competing with values. The vertical rhythm between elements — 12 px between cards, 16 px within card internal padding, 6 px between label and value — is unwavering. Rhythm creates trust. A system that holds its spacing exactly becomes legible in peripheral vision, as familiar as a beloved instrument panel. This consistency is the product of painstaking iteration, of someone who counted pixels across every state and refused to leave a single misalignment uncorrected.

---

## Composition and Flow

The reading path follows signal direction: top-to-bottom in list views mirrors the physical signal chain from transmitter to receiver. In the workflow editor, the node canvas traces the same left-to-right energy journey. Nothing fights the eye's natural movement — the interface is a current, not an obstacle. Summary values appear at the crown of each view, anchoring the reader with the answer before the evidence. Navigation is layered in three tiers: bottom tabs for context-switching between domains, header tabs for view-mode toggling within a domain, and floating action bars for ephemeral tool modes. Each tier has its own visual weight — the bottom nav is the quietest, the content is the loudest, and the header rests between. The result is a composition of master-level visual architecture, where every relationship between element and element has been deliberated, reconsidered, and refined.

---

## Material and Precision

The surface is white paper — no gradients, no textures, nothing that competes with the data. Light falls evenly. The only decoration allowed is a 4-pixel left accent bar on each node card: a thin ribbon of color that identifies the component type before the eye reaches the badge or the name. This restraint is itself a design statement — the interface trusts its content to carry interest, requiring no embellishment. Every interactive surface carries a hover and press state that is barely perceptible at rest but unmistakable in motion, like a precision instrument acknowledging your grip. The entire system has been assembled with the care of a master watchmaker: each part in exactly its place, each tolerance held, each surface finished until the whole feels inevitable rather than designed.
